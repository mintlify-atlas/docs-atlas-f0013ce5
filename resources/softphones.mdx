## Overview

A softphone (combination of *software* and *telephone*) is software used for VoIP communications, whether between softphones or to hardphones (physical VoIP phones). They are very useful because you can test locally, without the need for additional hardware (which usually has a high price), the functionality of the PBX.

## Configuration Example

When configuring a softphone to connect to your Asterisk server, you'll typically need:

- **Server/Domain:** Your Asterisk server IP address (e.g., 192.168.1.180)
- **Username:** Your SIP extension (e.g., javier, belen)
- **Password:** The secret configured in `sip.conf`
- **Port:** 5060 (default SIP port)
- **Transport:** UDP, TCP, or TLS (depending on your configuration)

<Note>
  For secure calling with TLS, you'll need to import the certificate files (`.pem` and `.crt`) into your softphone. Blink is particularly user-friendly for certificate-based authentication.
</Note>

## Testing Recommendations

1. **Start Simple:** Begin with basic UDP connections before implementing TLS
2. **Use Multiple Clients:** Test with at least two different softphones to verify compatibility
3. **Mobile Testing:** Test mobile clients on actual networks (not just WiFi) to verify NAT traversal
4. **Feature Testing:** Not all softphones support all features (messaging, call transfer, etc.)

<Tip>
  ZoiPer and Blink are the most reliable options for testing advanced Asterisk features including voicemail, call transfer, and secure calling.
</Tip>
